# Lifecycle

> "Nothing exists forever. Everything must justify its continued existence."

---

# Purpose

Renegade is not a static program.

It is a deterministic symbolic architecture that is expected to grow, reorganize, mature, and simplify throughout its lifetime.

Every component—whether a capability, abstraction, memory, hypothesis, or organizational structure—has a lifecycle.

Existence alone is never justification.

Continued usefulness is.

---

# Core Principle

Everything begins small.

Nothing begins complete.

Every part of the architecture is expected to evolve.

Growth is encouraged.

Stagnation is questioned.

Decay is accepted.

Retirement is respected.

---

# The Universal Lifecycle

Every component follows the same progression.

```
Idea
    ↓
Prototype
    ↓
Experiment
    ↓
Validation
    ↓
Trusted
    ↓
Optimization
    ↓
Consolidation
    ↓
Maturity
    ↓
Decline
    ↓
Archive
    ↓
Retirement
```

Not every component reaches every stage.

Some fail immediately.

Some mature.

Some become foundational.

Some are eventually replaced.

---

# Existence Must Be Earned

Nothing receives permanent status.

Every capability must continuously justify:

- usefulness
- correctness
- transferability
- maintainability
- simplicity

Historical importance alone is insufficient.

---

# Growth

Growth is not measured by file count.

Growth is measured by improved reasoning.

Examples include:

- fewer assumptions
- greater transfer
- clearer organization
- simpler implementations
- broader applicability
- lower execution cost

Growth without improvement is expansion.

Expansion alone is not progress.

---

# Consolidation

As understanding improves, duplication should disappear.

The architecture should seek opportunities to:

- merge similar capabilities
- unify repeated concepts
- compress equivalent reasoning
- simplify execution paths
- reduce unnecessary complexity

The best architecture often contains fewer moving parts than the one before it.

---

# Decline

Decline is natural.

A component may become less useful because:

- a better abstraction exists
- broader reasoning replaces it
- dependencies disappear
- execution becomes inefficient
- its original assumptions no longer hold

Decline is information.

Not failure.

---

# Archival

Archiving preserves history.

Archived components retain:

- provenance
- execution history
- supporting evidence
- failure history
- relationships

Archived components are inactive.

They remain available for inspection, restoration, and historical understanding.

---

# Retirement

Retirement ends active participation.

A retired component no longer influences reasoning.

Its existence remains part of the architecture's history.

Removing operational responsibility is not equivalent to erasing history.

---

# Resurrection

Archived components may return.

Only evidence may justify restoration.

Nostalgia is not evidence.

---

# Foundational Components

Some components become foundational.

Foundational components are expected to remain stable because other systems depend upon them.

Even foundational components remain reviewable.

No component is beyond improvement.

---

# Evolution

Evolution should produce:

- greater generality
- greater clarity
- greater composability
- greater transfer
- lower complexity

Evolution should never produce unnecessary complication.

---

# Organizational Evolution

Not only capabilities evolve.

Entire organizational structures may evolve.

Directories.

Hierarchies.

Knowledge graphs.

Memory organization.

Planning systems.

Execution strategies.

The architecture must remain capable of reorganizing itself without losing its identity.

---

# Death

Deletion is the final option.

Deletion should occur only when a component is:

- irrecoverably incorrect
- harmful
- duplicated with complete preservation elsewhere
- intentionally removed after preserving its history

Whenever practical:

archive first.

Delete later.

---

# The Living Architecture

Renegade is intended to behave less like software frozen in time and more like a living symbolic organism.

Living systems grow.

Living systems adapt.

Living systems reorganize.

Living systems simplify.

Living systems survive by changing.

The architecture should do the same.

---

# Final Principle

Nothing is sacred except the principles that govern growth.

Everything else is expected to evolve.
