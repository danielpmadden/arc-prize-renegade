# CONCEPTS.md

## Purpose

Concepts are the fundamental building blocks of Renegade.

Capabilities execute.

Memory remembers.

Lineage relates.

Lifecycle governs.

Evidence supports.

Executive coordinates.

Concepts are what these systems operate upon.

Renegade does not ultimately reason about functions.

It reasons about concepts.

---

# Definition

A concept is a stable symbolic representation of some identifiable abstraction.

Examples include:

- Object
- Color
- Count
- Shape
- Adjacency
- Inside
- Symmetry
- Reflection
- Movement
- Sequence
- Difference
- Equality

Concepts are not limited to ARC.

They are intended to describe reality in general.

---

# Concepts are not words.

Names are labels.

Concepts are identities.

Changing a concept's label does not create a new concept.

Likewise,

multiple labels may refer to one concept,

and one label may ambiguously refer to multiple concepts.

Renegade reasons about concepts,

not vocabulary.

---

# Concepts are evidence-backed.

Every concept should ultimately possess evidence supporting its existence.

Evidence may include:

- observations
- experiments
- demonstrations
- successful execution
- verified reasoning
- human introduction

Evidence does not automatically validate a concept.

Evidence supports evaluation.

---

# Concepts possess identity.

Every concept has:

- stable identity
- lifecycle
- lineage
- provenance

Concepts are first-class entities.

They are not anonymous dictionary keys.

---

# Concepts may relate.

Examples include:

contains

part_of

adjacent_to

causes

supports

contradicts

depends_on

analogous_to

derived_from

composed_of

intersects

generalizes

specializes

Relationships are themselves meaningful knowledge.

---

# Concepts may compose.

Multiple concepts may combine to create larger concepts.

Example:

Square

+

Red

+

Border

↓

Red Square Border

Composition should preserve provenance whenever practical.

---

# Concepts may decompose.

Complex concepts may be separated into simpler concepts.

Example:

Reflection Across Vertical Axis

↓

Reflection

+

Vertical

+

Axis

The preferred representation depends upon explanatory power.

---

# Concepts possess uncertainty.

Concepts need not immediately become trusted.

Possible states include:

candidate

experimental

validated

trusted

deprecated

archived

retired

Concept confidence is determined by evidence,

not frequency.

---

# Concepts evolve.

Concepts may:

merge

split

specialize

generalize

be replaced

be retired

Their identity and lineage should preserve historical continuity.

---

# Concepts are reusable.

A concept introduced while solving one task should remain available to future reasoning if validated.

Renegade should avoid task-specific concepts whenever a broader abstraction exists.

---

# Concepts should remain minimal.

A concept should represent one coherent abstraction.

If explanation requires repeated conjunctions,

multiple concepts may be preferable.

---

# Concepts are compositional.

Intelligence emerges primarily through composition,

not through isolated concepts.

Large reasoning structures should form dynamically from many interacting concepts.

---

# Concepts precede capabilities.

Capabilities consume concepts.

Capabilities produce concepts.

Capabilities transform concepts.

Capabilities discover relationships among concepts.

Capabilities do not define concepts.

---

# Concepts are independent of benchmarks.

ARC,

scientific reasoning,

language,

robotics,

geometry,

planning,

and future domains

should all reuse the same conceptual substrate whenever possible.

Concepts should not be invented merely to solve one benchmark.

---

# Success

Renegade succeeds when increasingly diverse domains can be explained through increasingly reusable conceptual structures,

rather than through benchmark-specific rules.

Concepts are intended to become the primordial soup from which future reasoning emerges.
