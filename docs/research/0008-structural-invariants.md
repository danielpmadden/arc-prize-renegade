# Pass 8: Structural invariants

Relationships preserve individual exact facts; invariants provide a small, inspectable compression of repeated facts already present in that relationship layer. Keeping them separate maintains provenance: an invariant cites explicit relationships rather than re-reading raw supplied material.

A repeated structural regularity is not a concept. It does not name what members are, explain why they recur, predict an action, or create a hypothesis. The deterministic playground exposes this compressed structure for inspection while semantic association, interpretation, reasoning, learning, and ARC solving remain deferred.
